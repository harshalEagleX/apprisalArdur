For “summary” Appraisal Reports, include enough detail consistent with the intended use and pursuant to the
USPAP Appraisal Report option, to allow the user to understand the appraiser's opinions and conclusions of value
and marketability. Include:
comp search criteria, availability or scarcity of sales;

reasoning for selecting comps (especially distant, over-6-month sales, sales dissimilar to the subject, large or
numerous adjustments);
information or charts in support of the market trend conclusion;
adjustment support;
disclosure and analysis of adverse and beneficial conditions and externalities.
Unacceptable practices: merely stating “best sales used as comps.” Using inapplicable or meaningless boilerplate.
Merely stating “a $ adjustment was made” without explaining the support or method. Listing a list of adjustment
methods, you might have used. Merely citing “experience” on its own without explaining information or methods
relied upon.
For Appraisal Review Reports, explain your logic and reasoning for all disagreements with the work under review;
and explain the support for your own methods, analyses, opinions, conclusions, information, and value opinion
related to any disagreements or errors with the work under review.
Unacceptable practices: Merely making a statement of disagreement with no detail or support for the disagreement
(e.g., "Adjustments are too low", "GLA for comp X is not correct", "Comps are not the best", and other non-
supported statements). Making comments about the appraiser, rather than about the appraisal report under review.
